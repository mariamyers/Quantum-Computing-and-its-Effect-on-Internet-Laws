Quantum Computers harness the capability of doing millions to billions of dollars in damage if used in the wrong way.

One of the many cybersecurity concerns is the ability to potentially "unravel data encryption protocols. It could also potentially compromise gaps due to its ability to scan vast distances for nearby networked devices applications that are open" (Malonson, 2).

Quantum computing can possibly perform attacks on Grover's and Shor's alogorithms soon which threatens hash functions and public-key cryptography. To prevent this from happening blockchains must be redesigned to resist, called pos-quantum cryptosystems (Fernández-Caramès & Fraga-Lamas, 2020).
