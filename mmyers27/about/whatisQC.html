Quantum Computers are very powerful computers that operate by utilizing the quantum mechanical phenomena of superposition and entanglement that interacts with qubits (IBM, "What is Quantum Computing?").


Qubits are just like bits with values of 0 and 1 but they exist in superposition - meaning, they are both 0 and 1 until observed.


Superposition enables the ability to explore multiple options and process information in a fraction of the time compared to classical computers thus performing operations that are too complex for any normal computers (IBM, "What is Quantum Computing?").
