Currently, we only withhold a small fraction of the potential power of a quantum computer. In November of 2021, IBM revealed their newest invention: the Eagle, the world's most powerful quantum processor containing a whopping 127 quantum bits (qubits) (Irving, 2021).

Companies and countries are all racing to have the most advanced quantum computer. Back in 2019, Google was in the lead with their Sycamore processor which had 53 qubits. IBM plans to release the Osprey which harnesses 433 qubits. With each qubit added, the computer gets exponentially more powerful (Irving, 2021).
